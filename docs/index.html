Narnia Character Annotation

Claude Opus annotated all 17 chapters of The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe with zero task-specific training — no fine-tuning, no labeled examples, just a prompt. It identified 2,361 character mentions, resolved 43 characters from dozens of surface aliases, and classified each mention into one of six agency roles.

Method

Claude Opus was prompted chapter-by-chapter with label definitions and a small alias mapping. No examples from this text were used in training. The model returned structured CSV with character identities and agency labels per sentence.

Why this matters

Pre-built NLP pipelines (NER + coreference + rule-based classifiers) require task-specific engineering for each annotation schema. This experiment shows that a general-purpose LLM can handle novel, nuanced annotation schemas — including subtle distinctions like ACTIVE_THOUGHT vs ACTIVE_PERFORMER — out of the box, with consistent results across a 17-chapter narrative.
